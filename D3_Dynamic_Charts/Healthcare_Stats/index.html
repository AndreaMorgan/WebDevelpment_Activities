Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

The above chart shows data for each state to show possible correlations between a state's population in poverty and lack of healthcare, and median age and lack of healthcare.

From the distribution of states when looking at poverty level and lack of healthcare, the data looks as if it could be related. At first glance, the data appears to show that the higher a state's population in poverty, the higher percentage of the population lacks healthcare. Further statistical testing can confirm or negate this hypothesis.

On the other hand, the median age of the population of a state and the percentage of the population with a lack of healthcare does not appear to be related.
